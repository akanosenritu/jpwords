Japanese date expressions are like this:

* <Ruby t={"さきおととい"}>一昨々日</Ruby>: 3 days ago, seldom used.
* <Ruby t={"おととい"}>一昨日</Ruby>: the day before yesterday, 2 days ago.
* <Ruby t={"きのう"}>昨日</Ruby>: yesterday.
* <Ruby t={"きょう"}>今日</Ruby>: today. The expression of "こんにちは" (hello) comes from the abbreviation of "<Ruby t="こんにち">今日</Ruby>はご<Ruby t="きげん" >機嫌</Ruby>いががですか" (how are you today?).
* <Ruby t={"あした"}>明日</Ruby>: tomorrow.
* <Ruby t={"あさって"}>明後日</Ruby>: the day after tomorrow, 2 days later.
* <Ruby t={"しあさって"}>明々後日</Ruby>: 3 days later.

You can also use <Ruby t="にちまえ">日前</Ruby> and <Ruby t="にちご">日語</Ruby>.
* <Ruby t={"じゅうごにちまえ"}>十五日前</Ruby> (15 days ago)
* <Ruby t={"さんじゅうにちご"}>三十日後</Ruby> (30 days later)

Numbers 2 to 10 need special care reading them however, since for example "2日" is "ふつか", not "ににち".
* <Ruby t={"ふつかまえ"}>二日前</Ruby> (2 days ago)
* <Ruby t={"とおかご"}>十日後</Ruby> (10 days later)
