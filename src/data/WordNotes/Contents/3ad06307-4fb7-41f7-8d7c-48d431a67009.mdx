Japanese date expressions are like this:

* <Ruby b={"一昨々日"} t={"さきおととい"} />: 3 days ago, seldom used.
* <Ruby b={"一昨日"} t={"おととい"} />: the day before yesterday, 2 days ago.
* <Ruby b={"昨日"} t={"きのう"} />: yesterday.
* <Ruby b={"今日"} t={"きょう"} />: today. The expression of "こんにちは" (hello) comes from the abbreviation of "<Ruby b="今日" t="こんにち" />はご<Ruby b="機嫌" t="きげん" />いががですか" (how are you today?).
* <Ruby b={"明日"} t={"あした"} />: tomorrow.
* <Ruby b={"明後日"} t={"あさって"} />: the day after tomorrow, 2 days later.
* <Ruby b={"明々後日"} t={"しあさって"} />: 3 days later.

You can also use <Ruby b="日前" t="にちまえ" /> and <Ruby b="日後" t="にちご" />.
* <Ruby b={"十五日前"} t={"じゅうごにちまえ"} /> (15 days ago)
* <Ruby b={"三十日後"} t={"さんじゅうにちご"} /> (30 days later)

Numbers 2 to 10 need special care reading them however, since for example "2日" is "ふつか", not "ににち".
* <Ruby b={"二日前"} t={"ふつかまえ"} /> (2 days ago)
* <Ruby b={"十日後"} t={"とおかご"} /> (10 days later)
